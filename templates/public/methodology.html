    Methodology

    Understanding the Parametric Comparison Method (PCM).

    What is a Parameter?

    In the framework of Generative Grammar, a parameter is a binary switch (sets of Yes/No options) that defines the syntactic variability of a language.

    • Universal Grammar: The shared biological capacity for language.
    • Parameters: The specific settings that differentiate, for example, English from Japanese.

    Data Collection Process

    The PCM Hub collects data through a rigorous process:

    1. Elicitation

    Native speakers or experts answer binary questions designed to isolate specific syntactic rules.

    2. Review

    Data is reviewed by specific language experts to ensure accuracy and handle dialectal variation.

    3. Computation

    Algorithms (like KITSCH) calculate the distance between languages based on parameter differences.
